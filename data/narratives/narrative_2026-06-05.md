## What moved & why

### Equities & sectors

The story of June 4 is not captured by the index level — it is captured by the **1.93 percentage-point divergence between the Dow and the Nasdaq in a single session.**

**Broadcom (AVGO) −15%** erased approximately $300bn in market cap after the company declined to raise its AI revenue guidance for FY27 "in a market environment clamoring for material beats and raises" (MarketWatch, June 4; FT: "Broadcom suffers $300bn rout"). This follows CRM −5.1%, NVDA −3.6%, MSFT −3.2%, AMZN −2.5%, TSMC −2.2% in prior sessions. Five of the eight most important AI-infra names have now repriced in the same direction over one week. This is not company-specific noise; it is a market-wide recalibration of whether AI data-center capex growth can sustain the valuation expansion of 2024–25. XLK printed −2.41% — the worst sector on the day.

Capital rotated cleanly into the rest of the market:

- **Healthcare (XLV) +2.93%** — led by UHC (surging on regulatory news per Investing.com). The defensive rotation is clear.
- **Financials (XLF) +2.15%** — Visa +3.1%, Mastercard +2.84%. This is payments processing, not bank credit — the bid is for quality cash-flow compounders, not rate-sensitive lenders.
- **Meta +2.54%, Alphabet +2.52%** — ad-monetization names distinctly outperforming hardware AI names. The market is drawing a line between "AI as revenue driver" (META, GOOGL) and "AI as capex recipient" (NVDA, AVGO, MSFT).

Larry McDonald's warning (MarketWatch, June 4) that tech is "flashing a warning sign last seen in 2020" and investors are piling in thinking it's the "safe trade" when the rotation should be to hard assets is worth framing. XLK +33.2% YTD with the sector now in confirmed multiple compression, alongside 10Y yields at the 93.7th %ile, is precisely the duration-risk configuration that preceded the 2022 unwind. The setup is analogous; the catalyst (AI earnings disappointment rather than rate shock) is different.

**Asia followed:** Nikkei −1.36%, Hang Seng −1.48% (notably Hang Seng was a leader on June 3, +2.52%; this mean-reversion is clean). ASML −1.12% — European semiconductor names absorbing the AI-hardware de-rating.

**IPO market context:** Quantinuum (Honeywell's quantum spin-off) raised $1.68bn in its Nasdaq IPO (MarketWatch, June 4); SpaceX self-valued at $1.75tn (BBC) ahead of its retail debut via Fidelity. Trillion-dollar AI IPOs opening alongside a tech de-rating is the classic late-cycle supply dynamic — the SoFi strategist calling it a market-reshaping event (Seeking Alpha) is precisely the kind of narrative that peaks at cycle turns.

## Risk lens

**AI capex cycle is now repricing, not rotating.** Five major AI-infra names down >2% in the same week, culminating in AVGO −15%, is a cycle signal. XLK at +33.2% YTD carries the dominant factor risk in the S&P 500. The multiple compression in tech (10Y at 93.7th %ile as the discount rate) plus the earnings-cycle deceleration (AVGO declining to raise guidance) is the two-sided pressure that caused the 2022 tech drawdown. This cycle's version has the additional feature of private credit stress, which 2022 did not.

**CFTC positioning (May 26 — stale 9 trading days):**
- S&P e-mini lev net: **−457,780 contracts** (the lev_net_chg was −56,226 the prior week — shorts were still adding heading into this data). The squeeze potential is enormous.
- Nasdaq lev net: **−51,679 contracts**
- VIX lev net: **−49,336 contracts** (short vol into an expanding vol premium — the classic unhedged position)
- Ultra T-Bond lev net: **−871,013 contracts** (large short-duration bet; covering = rate rally)
- Ultra 10Y lev net: **−239,726 contracts**

The spec-short complex in equities AND the short-vol complex are positioned for continued calm. A correlated shock (weak payrolls + private credit spread widening + tech continuation) would force all of these to cover simultaneously.

**Vol risk premium expanded to +6.9** (VIX 15.8 / 20d realized 8.8), from +6.2 prior session. The realized vol dropped as single-stock vol stays disaggregated — the index is suppressed by the dispersion trade while individual names gap violently (AVGO −15%, CRM −5.1% etc.). The index-level calm is a measurement artifact; it will resolve violently when correlations snap back.

**Portfolio hedge map is deteriorating:**
- Duration doesn't work (stock-bond correlation still positive; 10Y at 93.7th %ile means treasuries are not safe havens in this regime)
- Energy lost its hedge function (WTI −3.0% on June 4; XLE flat)
- Credit too tight to add defensive protection (OAS at 3rd %ile = no room to compress further as a long)
- Gold is working (structural bid confirmed), but at the 64th %ile it is not optically cheap
- Cash (T-bills 3.62%) remains the cleanest carry trade available

**The Warsh/tariff combination** creates a Fed that won't rescue a growth scare. A soft payroll today does NOT guarantee Fed cuts if Warsh is hawkish and tariffs are re-stoking inflation. The "bad news is good news" (weak data = rate cuts) framework may be breaking down.

**What to watch:**

1. **8:30 AM ET today — May payrolls.** Below 130k + cooling wages = spec-short S&P squeeze, likely tech-led rebound; above 200k + hot wages = 10Y back toward 4.5%, tech compression deepens. 130k–200k with steady wages = worst outcome for positioning — no clean catalyst to resolve either direction.
2. **HY OAS for any uptick.** With two private credit gates, the first 1–2bp move in OAS is the leading indicator of cascade. Trigger: any close above 2.80%.
3. **Third private credit gate this week.** Apollo, Ares, Starwood — any additional cap or suspension in the next 5 sessions converts this to a 2022-style episode. Watch for peer announcements.
4. **AVGO's post-open trajectory.** Does it stabilize at −15% or extend? An analyst upgrade-driven recovery above −8% on Friday would signal "buy the bad guidance" — a resilience signal for the AI capex thesis. No recovery means capitulation is underway.
5. **Copper below $6.20.** At the 98.4th %ile, copper is the single most stretched asset. A crack in copper while oil is weak and tech is selling would be the cleanest "global growth scare" signal in the distribution.
