## What moved & why

### Commodities & credit

The session's dominant cross-asset signal: **WTI −$3.09 (−3.2%) to $92.44; Brent −$2.99 (−3.1%) to $94.36.** Both benchmarks have slipped in their 1-year distribution — WTI from the 86th to 81st percentile, down from yesterday's elevated level. The move happened overnight (pre-US open), pointing to Asia/European-hours drivers — most likely OPEC+ production chatter, demand-side revision, or large-fund position unwind. The key analytical question is *why gold rose simultaneously:* **Gold +$55.30 (+1.24%) to $4,519.70** while DXY fell −0.31 to 99.24 and WTI dropped. This combination is inconsistent with a simple Iran-risk-off. It is consistent with (a) sustained geopolitical uncertainty (Middle East premium persists in safe-haven demand even as crude lightens) or (b) emerging growth skepticism driving flight to gold. Copper's resilience — **+0.62% to $6.52, 98th 1-year %ile** — argues against a clean "demand-destruction" read, since copper is the hardest cyclical tell. The most likely read: **leveraged long oil positions unwinding** (Iran risk premium deflation), while gold absorbs the "I don't trust this resolution" flow and copper hasn't caught the memo yet.

Public credit: **HYG flat at $79.68 (95th %ile); HY OAS unchanged at 2.71% (3rd %ile); IG OAS crept +1bp to 0.74% (3rd %ile).** The public credit market sees nothing. The private credit market has just shown a gate — Blackstone capped withdrawals from its flagship private credit vehicle as Q2 redemption requests surged to **$4.5bn** (FT, June 4). This is a liquidity event in the slow-moving, mark-to-model layer of credit. Public OAS spreads are the last thing to move; gates come first.

If the market is recalibrating capex cycle expectations, the multiple compression has further to run: XLK sits at YTD +36.5%, with 10Y yields at the 96th %ile compressing the discount rate for those growth premiums simultaneously. The combined pressure of higher-for-longer rates AND capex-cycle deceleration is precisely what consensus didn't price for.

**Private credit gate is the slow-moving risk.** Blackstone's $4.5bn Q2 redemption surge in its flagship private credit vehicle is a structural stress signal, not a market-risk one. Public credit (HY OAS 2.71, 3rd %ile) remains calm. The historical pattern: private credit gates in late 2022 appeared ~3–4 weeks before IG/HY spreads started widening materially. This is not an imminent public spread event, but it is the first visible stress in the credit complex since the cycle began.

**Portfolio hedge map is now worse:** (1) duration doesn't work (stock-bond corr +0.76); (2) energy just lost its hedge function (WTI −3.2%); (3) credit too tight to add defensive protection. Cash (T-bills at 3.62%, EFFR 0th %ile = lowest of the year, but absolute carry is there) and possibly gold (if the safe-haven bid holds) are the only clean defensive plays.
