## What moved & why

### Equities & sectors
The tape is a rotation, not a selloff. Leaders **ASML +4.72% / TSMC +2.54% / Nikkei +2.50%** (AI-capex semis) sit beside laggards **Salesforce −4.18%, Microsoft −4.17%, Alphabet −3.86%, Mastercard −3.55%**. Sector map confirms it: **Utilities +1.86%, Materials +1.18%, Energy +1.15%, Industrials +1.04%, Tech +1.25%** (semi-led) up; **Comm. Services −1.76%** (Alphabet/Meta drag), Health −0.97%, Discretionary −0.51% down. Russell 2000 (+0.90%) beating the S&P says the *median* stock had a fine day — the index looks flat only because its biggest names are the ones rolling over.

### Rates & the dollar
Yields rose across the curve — **5Y +4bp to 4.22%, 10Y +4bp to 4.49%, 30Y +2bp to 4.99%** — with the front leading. That's the mechanical driver of the mega-cap growth crack: long-duration multiples (MSFT, GOOGL, CRM) are the most rate-sensitive, and a 10Y pushing back toward 4.5% is exactly where they hurt. The **dollar firmed (DXY +0.24%)**, EUR/USD soft — consistent with higher US real yields.

### Commodities & credit
**Energy is the geopolitical bid and the growth tax in one:** WTI **$95.99 (+2.38%, +67% YTD)**, Brent $97.99, on the Iran headlines. Meanwhile **metals went the other way** — Gold −0.04% to $4,487, Silver −1.22%, **Copper −1.12%** — so this is an *energy-specific* move, not a broad commodity surge (copper falling argues against a clean global-growth reflation). Credit is calm: **HY OAS 2.72%, IG 0.73%**, HYG +0.08% — no stress signal.

## Risk lens

**Positioning read:** this is a healthy-looking rotation *for now* — breadth broadened, small caps led, credit is tight. The danger is that the market's only two engines are **semis (ASML/TSMC)** and **energy**; mega-cap growth, payments (V/MA), and comm-services have all rolled. If semis wobble, the index loses its last leadership and the "flat" tape turns into a down one fast.

**Correlations to watch:** the textbook stock↓/bond↑ hedge is **not** working — yields rose *with* equity weakness in growth. That's a duration-unwind signature, and it means Treasuries aren't cushioning a growth drawdown. Watch whether that persists.

**Tail risks:**
1. **Long-end break.** 10Y through ~4.5–4.6% accelerates the growth/duration unwind — the MSFT/GOOGL/CRM crack is the preview.
2. **Iran → oil spike → stagflation.** WTI +8% on the week is already a growth tax; an escalation that takes crude to $105+ pairs badly with core CPI at 2.75%.
3. **Leadership collapse.** The rally narrowed to semis + energy; if either breaks, there's no bench.

**What to watch next (next session grades this):**
- Does the **rotation hold** — do semis/energy/cyclicals keep leadership, or do mega-caps bounce and re-narrow the tape?
- **10Y at 4.49%** — toward 4.6% (growth pain) or back under 4.4% (relief)?
- **WTI ~$96** and the Iran headlines — escalation or de-escalation?
- **HY OAS 2.72%** — the calm-signal. If it widens, "rotation" becomes "de-risking" and the whole read flips.
- The **June Fed dot plot** and the Warsh-abstention wrinkle.
