# Global Financial Crisis (2007–2009)

## What happened
A decade of ultra-loose money and a housing bubble built a mountain of subprime mortgages,
repackaged into AAA-rated securities (MBS/CDOs) and levered across the global banking system.
As home prices fell from their 2006 peak, those securities became unvaluable. The unwind:
- **Aug 2007** — two Bear Stearns credit funds collapse; interbank funding (LIBOR-OIS) seizes.
- **Mar 2008** — Bear Stearns fails; JPMorgan buys it in a Fed-backstopped deal.
- **Sep 2008** — Fannie/Freddie nationalized; **Lehman Brothers files for bankruptcy (Sep 15)** —
  the moment the crisis went systemic; AIG bailed out the next day; money-market funds "break
  the buck"; commercial paper freezes.
- **Oct 2008–Mar 2009** — global equities crash; the S&P 500 falls **~57% from its Oct-2007 peak
  to its Mar-9-2009 low (~666)**.

## The Fed's decisions (Bernanke)
- Cut the funds rate from **5.25% (mid-2007) to 0–0.25% by Dec 2008** — the start of the
  Zero Interest-Rate Policy (**ZIRP**) era.
- Invented alphabet-soup liquidity facilities (TAF, TSLF, PDCF, CPFF, AMLF) to backstop funding.
- Launched **Quantitative Easing (QE1, Nov 2008→2010)**: bought ~$1.75T of Treasuries and MBS —
  the first time the Fed expanded its balance sheet to push down long rates directly.
- Acted as lender of last resort to the whole shadow-banking system, not just banks.

## The government's decisions
- **TARP (Oct 2008, $700B)** — Treasury injected capital into banks (and autos), guaranteed debt.
- The Fed/Treasury/FDIC backstopped money-market funds and bank debt.
- **ARRA (Feb 2009, ~$830B)** — the Obama fiscal stimulus (tax cuts, infrastructure, aid to states).
- **Stress tests (May 2009)** and Dodd-Frank (2010) reformed bank capital and oversight.

## What it did to the US
- Worst recession since the Depression: unemployment peaked at **10% (Oct 2009)**, ~8.7M jobs lost.
- The housing collapse wiped out trillions in household wealth; a wave of foreclosures.
- Began a decade of zero rates, QE, and a slow, low-inflation recovery (see **zirp-qe**).
- Politically: the bailouts seeded both the Tea Party and Occupy Wall Street.

## Read it in the data (the timeline)
- **HY credit spreads (hy_oas)** blew out toward ~20% at the peak of the panic (vs ~3–5% normal) —
  the single clearest crisis gauge. (Our timeline's HY series only starts 2023, but this is the
  canonical "credit is the line" lesson the daily read leans on.)
- **VIX** spiked to ~**80** (Nov 2008) — its all-time closing high until COVID.
- **2s10s curve** had *inverted* in 2006 ahead of the crisis, then steepened violently as the Fed cut.
- **S&P** −57% peak to trough; the dollar (DXY) caught a flight-to-safety bid.

## Lessons / echoes today
- **Credit spreads, not equities, are the early-warning system** — equities can drift while
  funding markets seize. (This is why the daily brief watches HY OAS so closely.)
- A yield-curve inversion led the recession by ~1–2 years.
- "Whatever it takes" central banking was born here and reused in COVID.
