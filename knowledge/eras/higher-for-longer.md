# Higher-for-longer (2023 → now)

## What happened
After the fastest hiking cycle in 40 years, the Fed reached a **~5.5% peak** and shifted to *holding*
rather than cutting — keeping policy **restrictive** to finish the job on inflation. The surprise has
been **"immaculate disinflation"**: CPI fell from ~9% toward the low-3s/high-2s **without a recession**,
defying the deeply **inverted 2s10s curve** that has warned of one since 2022. Unemployment stayed low,
the consumer held up, and the AI capex boom (Nvidia et al.) powered a narrow, mega-cap-led equity rally.
The central debate: **soft landing vs. a long-and-variable-lag recession that's merely delayed.**

## The Fed's decisions (Powell)
- **Hold at the peak**, data-dependent, leaning against premature easing; continue QT.
- The reaction function: cut only once inflation is *durably* near 2% — wary of a 1970s-style restart.
- Markets oscillate between pricing aggressive cuts and "higher-for-longer."

## The government's decisions
- Large fiscal deficits (~6–7% of GDP) *despite* a strong economy — heavy **Treasury issuance** that
  pressures long-end yields (the "supply" the brief watches). Debt-ceiling brinkmanship and the
  Aug-2023 Fitch downgrade of US debt.

## What it makes the current daily read about
- **Is this a duration/rates regime or a credit regime?** Yields near cycle highs, the curve at its
  flattest/most-inverted, credit spreads *historically tight* (priced for perfection), vol low — the
  "late-cycle, priced-for-perfection" picture the dashboard's percentiles surface every day.
- **Does the soft landing hold, or do the lags finally bite?** Watch credit (HY OAS) for the regime change.

## Read it in the data (timeline — this is the live era)
- **2s10s (curve_2s10s)**: deeply inverted, the 0th-percentile readings; watch for re-steepening.
- **ust10**: near multi-year highs (the duration headwind on long-multiple tech).
- **hy_oas**: near the *tight* extreme (3rd percentile) — the calm that flips the thesis if it widens.
- **stock-bond correlation**: still positive — the hedge that doesn't hedge.

## Lessons / open questions (you're living this one)
- Can a curve invert this deeply, this long, and *not* precede a recession? The current test.
- Tight credit + low vol into a higher-for-longer hold is a coiled spring — cheap-looking hedges, asymmetric risk.
