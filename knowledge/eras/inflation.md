# Post-COVID inflation & tightening (2021–2023)

## What happened
The collision of **massive stimulus + reopening demand + snarled supply chains + (in 2022) the
Russia/Ukraine energy shock** drove the worst inflation in 40 years. **CPI peaked at ~9.1% (June
2022).** The Fed, having called inflation "transitory" through 2021, pivoted hard and ran its
**fastest hiking cycle since the early 1980s** — from zero to **5.25–5.50%** in ~16 months — and
began **quantitative tightening (QT)**.
- **2022 was a brutal year for everyone**: the S&P fell ~25% peak-to-trough and bonds had one of
  their worst years ever — the classic 60/40 portfolio's hedge *failed* because **rates were the
  cause**, so stocks and bonds fell together (the same dynamic the daily brief flags as a "broken
  hedge").
- The **2s10s curve inverted deeply (2022)** — the market's recession warning.
- **Mar 2023**: rapid rate hikes broke **Silicon Valley Bank** and Credit Suisse (duration/funding
  risk) — a mini banking scare the Fed contained with a new facility (BTFP).

## The Fed's decisions (Powell)
- Held too long in 2021 ("transitory"), then hiked in **75bp clips** through 2022 — the most
  aggressive pace in a generation — and started QT (shrinking the ~$9T balance sheet).
- Re-anchored its credibility on the **Volcker precedent**: keep policy restrictive until inflation
  is durably beaten, even at the cost of growth.

## The government's decisions
- The **Inflation Reduction Act (Aug 2022)** (climate/health/tax — modest near-term inflation effect),
  the **CHIPS Act**, and the 2021 infrastructure law — large fiscal programs *into* a tightening cycle,
  an unusual monetary/fiscal tension.
- **SPR releases (2022)** to cap gasoline prices after the energy spike — the strategic buffer drawn
  down (echoed in today's EIA energy reads).

## What it did to the US
- A cost-of-living squeeze, a housing slowdown as mortgage rates hit ~7–8%, but — so far — **no
  recession**: the "immaculate disinflation" / soft-landing surprise that defines the *next* era
  (higher-for-longer).
- Ended the free-money era and the everything-bubble; rates "normalized" to pre-2008 levels.

## Read it in the data (timeline — directly visible)
- **2s10s (curve_2s10s)**: the deep 2022 inversion — the clearest recession signal in decades.
- **10y yield (ust10)**: the surge from ~1.5% (2021) toward ~4–5%.
- **VIX**: elevated through 2022 but never a true panic — an *orderly* bear, not a crash.
- **stock-bond correlation**: turned positive (the hedge broke) — the defining risk feature.

## Lessons / echoes today
- When **rates are the driver**, bonds stop hedging stocks — diversification fails exactly when
  you need it (the live lesson in the current daily read).
- "Transitory" is a warning about anchoring to a prior; inflation expectations are fragile.
- A deeply inverted curve can persist for a *long* time before (or without) a recession — timing ≠ direction.
