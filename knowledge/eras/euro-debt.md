# European sovereign-debt crisis (2010–2012)

## What happened
The GFC exposed that several euro-zone governments had borrowed far more than they could repay —
but, sharing a currency, they couldn't devalue or print their own money. **Greece** revealed in
late 2009 it had hidden its deficit; its bonds collapsed. Contagion spread to **Portugal, Ireland,
Italy, Spain (the "PIIGS")**. The fear: a sovereign default cascading into the European banks that
held those bonds, and even a **euro break-up** (countries leaving and redenominating).
- Bailouts for **Greece (2010, 2012), Ireland (2010), Portugal (2011)**; Spain's banks (2012).
- Peripheral 10-yr yields spiked (Greek to >30%, Italian/Spanish toward 7%) while German Bunds
  hit record lows as a haven — a violent "fragmentation" of the single market.
- **Jul 26 2012 — ECB President Mario Draghi: "the ECB is ready to do whatever it takes to
  preserve the euro. And believe me, it will be enough."** Backed by the OMT bond-buying program,
  it broke the panic almost overnight — one of the most effective central-bank communications ever.

## The policy decisions
- **ECB**: emergency liquidity (LTROs), rate cuts, then **OMT** (unlimited conditional bond-buying,
  never actually used — the *promise* was enough). Later: negative rates (2014) and its own QE (2015).
- **EU/IMF "Troika"**: bailouts conditioned on harsh **austerity** (Greece's economy shrank ~25%).
- Steps toward **banking union** and the ESM permanent rescue fund.

## What it did to the US (the cross-Atlantic channel)
- Recurrent **risk-off shocks** hit US markets (notably summer 2010 and summer–autumn 2011) — the
  US recovery's biggest external threat in the ZIRP years.
- Reinforced the **dollar and Treasuries as the global safe haven**; flight-to-quality compressed
  US yields further.
- A live case study in **monetary union without fiscal union** — and in how one credible central-bank
  sentence can end a panic.

## Read it in the data (timeline)
- **VIX** spiked in **mid-2010** and again **Aug–Oct 2011** (paired with the US debt-ceiling fight) —
  the two big risk-off events of the recovery.
- **DXY** and **10y yield**: dollar-up / US-yields-down on each European flare (haven flows).

## Lessons / echoes today
- **A central bank's words can be its most powerful tool** — Draghi moved markets more with a
  sentence than with money.
- Currency unions concentrate risk where there's no fiscal backstop (a structural euro fragility).
- Contagion is about *who holds the bonds* — the sovereign-bank "doom loop."
