# COVID crash & response (2020–2021)

## What happened
The pandemic forced a near-total economic shutdown. Markets had the **fastest bear market in
history**: the **S&P 500 fell ~34% in ~33 days (Feb 19 → Mar 23 2020)**, with even Treasuries and
gold briefly selling off in a frantic "dash for cash." Then the largest combined policy response
ever staged a **V-shaped recovery** — the S&P made new highs by Aug 2020 while the real economy was
still on its knees ("the most-hated bull market").
- **VIX hit ~82.7 (Mar 16 2020)** — a new all-time closing high, edging out 2008.
- Oil briefly went **negative (WTI −$37, Apr 20 2020)** as storage filled.

## The Fed's decisions (Powell)
- Cut to **0–0.25% in two emergency moves (Mar 3 and Mar 15 2020)**.
- **Unlimited QE** ("as much as needed") + a blitz of facilities backstopping Treasuries, MBS, money
  markets, munis, and — for the first time — **corporate bonds** (including, via ETFs, high-yield).
  The balance sheet went from ~$4T to ~$7T in months, then toward ~$9T.
- Adopted **"average inflation targeting" (Aug 2020)** — a promise to *let inflation run hot* to make
  up for past misses. (This framework is widely blamed for the Fed being late on the 2021–22 inflation.)

## The government's decisions
- **CARES Act (Mar 2020, ~$2.2T)** — direct stimulus checks, expanded unemployment, the PPP for small
  business, aid to states and industries. Plus a December 2020 package and the **American Rescue Plan
  (Mar 2021, $1.9T)**.
- Total US fiscal support reached **~$5T+** — peacetime-unprecedented; the deficit and M2 money supply
  exploded.

## What it did to the US
- A brief, brutal recession, then a boom: huge stimulus + reopening + supply constraints set the stage
  for the **2021–22 inflation surge** (see inflation).
- Asset prices, housing, and speculative manias (meme stocks, crypto, SPACs) ripped on free money.
- Cemented the lesson that the **monetary + fiscal "bazooka"** could short-circuit a crash — and that
  it has an inflationary bill that comes due.

## Read it in the data (timeline — you can see all of this directly)
- **VIX**: the ~82 spike (Mar 2020), then a slow normalization through 2020–21.
- **S&P (spx)**: the −34% cliff and the V-shaped recovery to new highs.
- **vol_premium** and **spx_spec_net**: the de-risking into the crash and the re-leveraging after.
- **2s10s** steepened as the Fed pinned the front end and growth/inflation hopes lifted the long end.

## Lessons / echoes today
- Policy can stop a *liquidity* crash fast — but flooding money into a *supply*-constrained economy
  brings inflation with a lag (the bill arrived in 2022).
- Markets and the real economy can fully decouple when liquidity is the marginal driver.
