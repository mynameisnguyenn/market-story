# Liftoff & normalization (2015–2020)

## What happened
The Fed finally began unwinding the post-GFC emergency settings. After the **Dec 2015 "liftoff"**
(the first hike in nearly a decade), it raised rates gradually to **2.25–2.50% by Dec 2018** and
started **quantitative tightening (QT)** — shrinking the balance sheet — in 2017. Growth was steady,
unemployment fell to ~50-year lows, inflation stayed near 2%, and equities ground higher (boosted by
the 2017 Tax Cuts and Jobs Act). Then:
- **Q4 2018**: the combination of hikes + QT + trade-war fears caused a sharp **~20% S&P selloff**
  into Christmas. Powell pivoted.
- **2019**: the Fed cut three times ("mid-cycle insurance"), the **2s10s briefly inverted (Aug 2019)**
  flashing a recession warning, and a repo-market funding squeeze (Sep 2019) forced the Fed to restart
  balance-sheet growth ("not QE").

## The Fed's decisions (Yellen → Powell)
- Slow, telegraphed hikes; QT on "autopilot" — until markets revolted in late 2018 and the Fed learned
  (again) it couldn't fully escape the balance sheet without breaking funding markets.
- The 2019 pivot showed the **"Fed put"** was alive.

## The government's decisions
- **Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (Dec 2017)** — a large corporate/individual tax cut, fiscal stimulus into a
  late-cycle expansion. The **US–China trade war (2018–19)** and tariffs were the era's macro wildcard.

## What it did to the US
- The longest US expansion on record (2009–2020), record-low unemployment — ended abruptly by COVID.
- Proved that *exiting* easy policy is fraught: QT + hikes broke markets in 2018 and funding in 2019.

## Read it in the data (timeline)
- **VIX**: the Feb-2018 "Volmageddon" spike (short-vol blow-up) and the Q4-2018 selloff.
- **2s10s**: flattened through the hikes, briefly inverted Aug 2019.
- **10y yield (ust10)**: rose toward ~3.2% (2018), then fell back as the Fed pivoted.

## Lessons / echoes today
- Tightening cycles tend to "break" something (2018 equities, 2019 repo) before the Fed stops.
- A brief curve inversion is a *signal*, not a *clock* — COVID, not the inversion, ended this cycle.
