# Dotcom boom & bust (1998–2002)

## What happened
The internet's commercialization triggered a speculative mania in technology and "new economy"
stocks. Companies with no profits (or revenue) IPO'd to billion-dollar valuations on eyeballs
and "clicks." The **Nasdaq roughly tripled from 1998 to its March-10-2000 peak (~5,048)**, then
collapsed **~78% to ~1,114 by Oct 2002**. The S&P 500 fell ~49%. Pets.com, Webvan, and hundreds
of dot-coms went to zero.
- Greenspan's **"irrational exuberance"** warning came in *Dec 1996* — three years early.
- The **1998 LTCM** blowup + Russian default caused a mid-cycle scare and a Fed rescue/cuts,
  which arguably re-fueled the bubble.
- The bust overlapped **9/11 (Sep 2001)** and a mild **2001 recession** + accounting scandals
  (Enron Dec 2001, WorldCom 2002).

## The Fed's decisions (Greenspan)
- **Hiked to 6.5% by mid-2000** to cool the boom — the trigger that pricked the bubble.
- Then **cut aggressively from 6.5% to 1.75% across 2001** (11 cuts) as tech imploded and after 9/11,
  reaching **1% by 2003**. This ultra-low-rate response set up the *next* bubble (housing).

## The government's decisions
- **Bush tax cuts (EGTRRA 2001)** — fiscal stimulus into the slowdown.
- **Sarbanes-Oxley (2002)** — accounting/governance reform after Enron/WorldCom.
- The **repeal of Glass-Steagall (1999)** and the hands-off stance on derivatives (CFMA 2000)
  are often cited as planting seeds for the later GFC.

## What it did to the US
- ~$5T of market value erased; tech-heavy regions and 401(k)s hit hard.
- A mild recession but a "jobless recovery"; the Fed kept rates at 1% into 2004.
- Cultural reset: profitability mattered again — the survivors (Amazon −90%-and-back) became giants.

## Read it in the data (the timeline starts 1998 — you can see this directly)
- **S&P (spx)**: the 2000 peak, the long bleed to Oct 2002, the bottoming.
- **VIX**: structurally elevated 2001–2002, spiking around 9/11 and the 2002 lows.
- **2s10s curve**: the Fed's 2000 hikes flattened it; the 2001 cuts steepened it hard.

## Lessons / echoes today
- **A great technology ≠ a great stock at any price** — valuation and profitability matter
  (the AI parallel writes itself).
- The Fed pricks bubbles with hikes, then over-eases into the bust, sowing the next one
  (dotcom → housing). A recurring policy reflexivity.
