# Glossary

Quick definitions. The `/finance` tutor expands these and adds new ones as we go.

- **basis point (bp):** 1/100th of a percent. 25bp = 0.25%. A "4bp move in the 10Y" = 0.04%.
- **ZIRP:** Zero Interest-Rate Policy — the fed funds rate pinned at 0–0.25% (2008–15, 2020–22).
- **QE / QT:** Quantitative Easing / Tightening — the Fed buying / shedding bonds to ease / tighten
  when rates alone aren't the tool. See `topics/quantitative-easing.md`.
- **fed funds rate:** the Fed's main policy rate (overnight interbank). The lever it sets directly.
- **the Fed put:** the market belief that the Fed will cut / ease to rescue falling asset prices.
- **the dual mandate:** the Fed's legal goals — maximum employment *and* stable prices (~2% inflation).
- **OAS (option-adjusted spread):** a corporate bond's yield premium over Treasuries = default-risk
  pricing. **HY** = high-yield/junk; **IG** = investment-grade. See `topics/credit-spreads.md`.
- **2s10s / the curve:** 10-year yield minus 2-year. Inversion (< 0) is a classic recession signal.
  See `topics/yield-curve.md`.
- **VIX:** the 30-day implied volatility of the S&P 500 — the "fear gauge." ~12–15 calm, >30 stress,
  ~80 crisis (GFC, COVID).
- **realized vs implied vol:** realized = how much the market *actually* moved; implied (VIX) = what
  options *price*. VIX − realized = the **vol risk premium** (high = complacency / pricey hedges).
- **DXY:** the US Dollar Index — the dollar vs a basket of major currencies. A haven in risk-off.
- **CFTC / COT / leveraged funds:** the Commitments of Traders report; **leveraged funds** = hedge-fund
  / speculator positioning (vs **asset managers** = real money). Net short specs = squeeze fuel.
- **soft landing:** the Fed cooling inflation without causing a recession (the current debate).
- **basis of the daily read:** is a selloff a *duration/positioning unwind* (credit calm, rates up) or
  *genuine de-risking* (credit widening)? Credit is the line.
